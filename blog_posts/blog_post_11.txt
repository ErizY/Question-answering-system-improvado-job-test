Understanding how to read those KPIs and effectively track progress toward their completion is another. </p><p>Displaying all KPIs on a single dashboard helps you create a coherent data story to help teams reach and exceed their goals. Without the dashboard, it’s often hard to see the forest for the trees and confidently navigate through all objectives.</p><p>Ready to learn more about KPI dashboards and explore how to build one for yourself? Then, let’s dive in.</p><h2>Key Takeaways</h2><ul role="list"><li>KPI dashboards combine data from various sources and display key insights using visual elements such as charts and graphs.</li><li>Effective KPI dashboards are focused and goal-centric, including only the data end-users need to answer KPI-specific business queries.</li><li>Some key benefits of KPI dashboards include facilitating executive decision-making, identifying opportunities and underperformance, increasing team collaboration and transparency, and facilitating real-time data analysis at scale.</li><li>To create a KPI dashboard, first choose the KPIs and metrics to track. Then select the relevant data sources. Once you’ve identified these, visualize the data using relevant charts and graphs. Lastly, share the dashboard.</li><li>Follow best practices to build KPI dashboards that everyone trusts. Include only data relevant to the goals, use text and interactive elements effectively, be visually consistent, keep it simple, and use good-quality data.</li><li>Analytics tools like Improvado can automatically sync all your data from different sources, ensure all your data has satisfactory quality, and provide you with a KPI dashboard that will serve as a single source of truth for your teams.</li></ul><h2>What Are KPI Dashboards?</h2><div class="w-embed"><figure style="max-width:1600px" class="w-richtext-align-fullwidth w-richtext-figure-type-image"><div><img data-blink-src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/5a1eb87c9afe1000014a4c7d/641485b397229d7a1667f7d2_O5nUyHhAhnSVv4snOCsE1ZpaAsJix7zYiMxfHdMzaUjVPHsZgZojjxjLlZtKn-FofdA1K3UIH-3AAeHq4axxGLJtEuyCkxTY2eitXoyoOzRer8-2pSo69dXFG6eir0474m6SyRaU9KYy7hzKXUfPMec.png" alt="Improvado’s KPI dashboard template"></div></figure></div><p>KPIs or key performance indicators are measurements that track how well a company, department, team, or person is progressing toward a goal. In other words, they are success metrics calculated based on data from different sources. </p><p>KPI dashboards show progress on KPIs in a visual, easily digestible format to help inform business decisions. Real-time dashboards automate any manual data wrangling processes, allowing teams to focus on uncovering insights into their goal progress. </p><p>KPI dashboards are a necessary foundation for teams and companies that want to join the data-driven side. However, ensuring you use good-quality data to avoid false insights is vital.</p><h2>What Do KPI Dashboards Include?</h2><p>A KPI dashboard includes all the relevant data end users need to make data-driven decisions for ultimate goal achievement and to adjust their strategy on the go.</p><p>The included metrics depend on the dashboard’s audience. What’s their goal? What question do they want to have answered? Which KPIs are important for the audience to gauge performance? What data can best help them get actionable business insights?</p><p>Once you’ve determined these variables, choose all the relevant data you need to include in the dashboard. Use various visualization techniques, such as trend analysis over time and comparatives and benchmarks, to provide more context.</p><p>Also, add annotations to the visuals to help describe the data to the end user.</p><div class="w-embed"><figure style="max-width:1600px" class="w-richtext-align-fullwidth w-richtext-figure-type-image"><div><img data-blink-src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/5a1eb87c9afe1000014a4c7d/641485b2a70cadfc7c4bdf53_gr3oITflj3d08-i_16366e9-iQ6hC5eu9cCJ6ZZC_kj-lTFwvEHfPABBZQ72EwW16tFs-p6x_VhB6jcTlR25jmiee4NB0qof3RalGODfcB3yqvQpyTV_LnxLbRhEIRPeA_g2IYNcpFW11Cgr2zimdOs.png" alt="Improvado’s cross-channel KPI dashboard"></div></figure></div><h2>Key Business Benefits of KPI Dashboards</h2><p>Data is only useful when you can make sense of it. However, making sense of it gets increasingly complicated with the growing amount of data you collect. That’s what KPI dashboards help you with. Let’s review how exactly this is achieved.</p><h3>Facilitates effective executive decision-making</h3><p>With a KPI dashboard, executives don’t have to wait for different departments to report on their activities and performance. All of this information is stored in a single source of truth for the company’s KPIs.</p><p>Executives have a real-time view of each team’s performance and contribution to the organization’s bottom line. This means they can see which units and processes to focus on to achieve business goals faster.</p><h3>Improves operational efficiency and effectiveness</h3><p>KPI dashboards streamline organizational workflow, making it easy for employees to work efficiently.</p><p>Let’s say the marketing team needs data on the lead-to-customer conversion rate from the sales team or the customer satisfaction index score from the customer support team. In these cases, they can get this info from the KPI dashboard without waiting for someone to hand them the data, stopping the time-consuming back and forth between different parties.</p><h3>Increases collaboration and transparency between teams</h3><p>With a shared KPI dashboard of real-time data, teams can easily make decisions without waiting for another person’s input.</p><p>This democratizes data, fostering collaboration and accelerating communication, ultimately leading to better team alignment and happier employees.</p><h3>Facilitates real-time data analysis at scale</h3><p>The real-time delivery of KPI dashboards gives executives insights into business operations as they happen.</p><p>Using a dashboard&#x27;s interactive elements, they can drill down into each specific KPI without going through many spreadsheets or wading through different programs.</p><h3>Provides valuable data for predicting business growth</h3><p>KPIs tell you how you&#x27;re progressing toward a goal. With a KPI dashboard, you have a tool that helps you assess the direction your business is heading.</p><p>You can better see patterns and forecast business growth—and with that, decide the next steps that will allow you to reach your business objectives efficiently.</p><h3>Provides insights on what to prioritize</h3><p>Data overwhelm is a huge problem for many decision-makers. Having dashboards on relevant KPIs—fueled by <a href="https://improvado.io/blog/what-is-data-quality" target="_blank">good-quality data</a>— means you can better analyze business operations without being overwhelmed.</p><p>Since dashboards properly organize and visualize critical information, you can quickly identify what to focus on and prioritize.</p><h2>Steps for Creating KPI Dashboards</h2><p>Developing a KPI dashboard is an intricate and time-consuming activity. But, not for those who know the right steps and use the right tools to visualize KPIs. </p><h3>Choose the KPIs and metrics to track</h3><p>What questions do end-users want to have answered and what information do they need? Consider this when creating a KPI dashboard, so you don&#x27;t end up featuring <a href="https://improvado.io/blog/what-is-a-vanity-metric" target="_blank">vanity metrics</a>.</p><p>Focus only on the most crucial metrics for the monitored activity. For example, if you’re building a sales pipeline dashboard, you definitely don’t need to include CLTV or CPA, because salespeople have almost nothing to do with these metrics. </p><p>Most KPI dashboards use the <a href="https://resources.useready.com/blog/mad-framework-for-actionable-dashboards/" target="_blank">MAD framework</a>—a three-level approach to creating dashboards. It includes the monitor level for high-level KPIs like annual recurring revenue or relative market share, the analysis level for analyzing KPIs across various departments, and the drill level for detailed KPIs for deep-dive analysis.</p><h3>Select relevant data sources</h3><p>Evaluate all your available data sources and decide which ones are relevant.           props = {'itIp': window.ip}

            Object.assign({}, props, userProps)
          );
      }
    }
  }
