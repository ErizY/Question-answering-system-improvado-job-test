This way, you can assess whether interest in your online store is growing or if you need to <strong>expand your marketing and advertising to increase traffic.</strong></p><p>Let’s take a look at Improvado’s cross-channel dashboard. It displays all vital metrics about your marketing performance, enabling you to see the performance of each campaign and channel in a single tab. Besides, you can monitor metrics broken down by gender, age, and geographical regions.</p><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" style="max-width:1274pxpx" class="w-richtext-align-fullwidth w-richtext-figure-type-image"><div><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/5a1eb87c9afe1000014a4c7d/641332524cf3e686d1ef59dc_2LGSKRUupMT04JzLlSTY_aWDbfUfLWO0ZvMvG6oBuBPgincWlZShPQk3Ez-FvUb5470oMCckKBUgxuSf5ym_3YOKjttK__eeB8QNBvSTbueDKdDnGN_87CAlDPfvy1DI7nC3GPurrlpGjvj1imf51w.png" alt=" Improvado’s paid ads dashboard : overview ‍"/></div></figure><p>For marketers interested solely in web traffic overview, <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/pradeepkumargpk/" target="_blank">Pradeep Kumar G</a> shared a comprehensive web traffic dashboard. It shows every traffic-related metric, from the total number of sessions to the best-performing referral domains.</p><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" style="max-width:1484pxpx" class="w-richtext-align-fullwidth w-richtext-figure-type-image"><div><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/5a1eb87c9afe1000014a4c7d/64133d7f932d0e0ac0ca9e47_bF23ILaP0oydpWQaTT0eGsI9s082ebLH5yqcc2xNdrivJreqsh610T-7_U-cnWFyiYJRpFhVSROamc_hWK12MYXVHEmFyPLrEQhLjn6Fg102tN3Q4ZwpRFn24YQ5O5eIF2B6S_oCQGV4.png" alt="Web traffic dashboard example‍"/></div></figure><h3>Bounce rate</h3><p>Bounce rate informs you what percentage of all sessions ended up viewing only a single page. To put it simpler, it shows you what part of your visitors wasn’t satisfied with your online store, at least as much to go check other pages than the one they’ve landed on. According to <a href="https://www.oberlo.com/statistics/average-bounce-rate-ecommerce">Oberlo</a>, the average bounce rate for e-commerce companies reached 47% in Q2 of 2022. </p><h3>User Behavior</h3><p>It&#x27;s not exactly a metric but rather a set of different metrics that helps you find the most promising customers and optimize user journeys, listings, and more. </p><p>Here’s an example of a Shopify dashboard that shows the breakdown of customers by geographical region, types of purchased products, purchase frequency, and more. </p><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" style="max-width:1022pxpx" class="w-richtext-align-fullwidth w-richtext-figure-type-image"><div><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/5a1eb87c9afe1000014a4c7d/64133daefbeda42f6dbf63f1_VEmEUo-SSjrZvSfgvI8CjAZ_h5q1-Q7QItzixwsTnAzevL7YLCrwC5oBQi1e2MMxN_Ix6CWzmsbeVo26Wu48gmKct22YDtkOj3zjbQ3LYub3DjeLQG4rfQhz1qaREzkgaL6JyTifObLv.png" alt="Shopify customer journey dashboard‍"/></div></figure><h3>Add to cart rate and abandoned cart rate</h3><p>Add to cart rate is the percentage of users that added some products to the cart during their visit. The other metric tells you what part of users abandon their carts and leave your e-commerce website without finalizing their transactions.</p><p>The add-to-cart rate depends heavily on the type of goods you’re selling. <a href="https://www.oberlo.com/statistics/add-to-cart-conversion-rate" target="_blank">Recent statistics from Oberlo</a> show that everyday goods and groceries have a higher add-to-cart rate compared to luxurious items or large goods, such as furniture.  </p><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" style="max-width:1600pxpx" class="w-richtext-align-fullwidth w-richtext-figure-type-image"><div><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/5a1eb87c9afe1000014a4c7d/64133253b7596d20efb5d041_72pZiptGKGYxPLTugoRV4VgGNGgceosCYynrlZyED3EjvWekiP_BCV608hsdrCklBmqXO21wfFieiNKZ2KUFKh83zqKuvXL5MLBCXe8d-BTflo8xLr-kL4Jt8SnsPWhhbefJ7ANzbX62.png" alt="Add-to-Cart rate benchmark by industry"/></div></figure><p>As for cart abandonment, the average rate for the retail industry stands at 72.8%. Yet again, the rate varies depending on the type of product you’re selling. According to Bolt, fashion goods have the highest cart abandonment rate of 40%.</p><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" style="max-width:1600pxpx" class="w-richtext-align-fullwidth w-richtext-figure-type-image"><div><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/5a1eb87c9afe1000014a4c7d/64133253bbb93d3115f3d473_OIRVco00IaiWITqg5ChjAc3x_InwDJhyo-oFkLRuR3U6eMLN9YN2OtV9_lEvcPrA67NqWeNziFEQHh-rZL-s6Nv6rwtlbP_fgEId30odvkPYQHqi4cUkMJ0b_BBOo6GpKWDKqVRUNLRE.png" alt="Cart abandonment rate stats by industry"/></div></figure><p>A Shopify sales dashboard helps you understand whether your shop is in line with the industry’s benchmarks, why users abandon their carts, and what are the points of improvement. </p><h3>Conversion rates and total sales</h3><p>You should analyze the conversion rate to learn if a proper percentage of visitors take the desired action on your store’s website. The goal may be signing in to the newsletter database or making a purchase. </p><p>Since buying is a widespread, final-stage conversion, you need to keep an eye on goals higher up the funnel to understand the percentage of users that become actual buyers. How many people that subscribed to your newsletter eventually became customers? What’s the percentage of users that made a purchase after a cart abandonment email? A well-designed Shopify dashboard should answer all of these questions.</p><h3>Average order value</h3><p>Average order value (AOV) helps sales specialists and marketers to define their next goals, devise a strategy and leverage discounts or recommendation systems. If it is too small, a company can offer additional products for an extra price or grant free delivery for orders above a given value. </p><p>AOV is calculated by dividing total revenue by the number of orders. However, for more detailed visualization, you can calculate the average order value by country or region. Here’s an example: </p><figure class="w-richtext-figure-type-image w-richtext-align-fullwidth" style="max-width:1600pxpx" class="w-richtext-align-fullwidth w-richtext-figure-type-image"><div><img src="https://assets-global.website-files.com/5a1eb87c9afe1000014a4c7d/64133252f33ac909211085e2_1JyhoN2hx8TfGctLUYGYyUYb0OZ58KpB4Bm_XQZfdbTQ3z8kVIQi2d5YIapFSdYUX4FZON9IGohpyZAmCoOJ_bnQIPuaD6F_-iWBYjoW1ZK_KJ3QqQNYMp14Aiw0xY7iSFYSiq7a719Z.png" alt=" Average order value by region dashboard example"/></div></figure><h3>New vs. returning customers and customer lifetime value</h3><p>It is essential to know how many of your visitors are new customers who have returned to buy again. You can track your clients’ behavior and assess their loyalty. To learn if your acquisition campaigns are cost-effective, you can check your customer acquisition cost. </p><p>The Customer Lifetime Value (CLV) helps you to measure the profitability of your customers (how much money they are willing to spend during your entire relationship with your business) and allows you to better adjust personalized campaigns. </p><p>Here’s a formula to help you calculate CLV: </p><p>Average customer lifetime value = (average number of transactions per month) * (average order value) * (average gross margin) * (average customer lifespan) / number of clients for the particular period.</p><p>There’s a vast space for experiments when displaying customer lifetime value. For example, you can find the most profitable customers by breaking down audiences into groups based on different criteria (sales channel, education, location, etc.). </p><h3>Online Store Sessions</h3><p>This metric allows you to learn how much time visitors spend on your website.<strong> </strong>A session starts when users visit your store and ends when they leave<strong> </strong>(the session is also considered ended if a visitor doesn&#x27;t interact with content for 30 minutes). In general, if the session is short, it can mean that the visitor is unsatisfied with the website (because he can’t find a particular product or find the website boring or non-intuitive, etc.). </p><p>According to different benchmarks, the median value for average session duration for B2C companies is 92.33 seconds.</p><p>Online store sessions are considered a tricky metric thought because it is difficult to decide how long the session should last. </p><h3>Inventory level</h3><p>In business, capital should flow. A high inventory level can mean that your capital is frozen. <strong>Information about inventory levels in specific periods, presented on the Shopify dashboard, can be used to adjust the store’s offer to the market’s demand.</strong> </p><p>Of course, inventory level also helps to manage products by making orders at the right time and in the right amounts. </p><h3>Shipping costs</h3><p>Online store owners should make sure that products are being shipped efficiently. <strong>With access to such data in your Shopify dashboard, you can react fast when you realize that you overspend on shipping and delivery. </strong></p><p>There are various ways to<strong> </strong>handle situations like that – you can negotiate new terms with your partners,<strong> </strong>increase the cost of delivery or raise the minimal value of the order for your customers.</p><p>Grow Your Shopify Account with Improvado </p><p>Thanks to <a href="https://improvado.io/integrations/shopify" target="_blank">Improvado integrations with Shopify</a> and 500+ marketing, sales, and analytics platforms, you can build a Shopify dashboard to serve as a universal command center of your store. <!-- Track any form -->
<script>
  function isFirstTimeVizit() {
  	if (window.mixpanel) {
      return !mixpanel.get_property('UserWasCreated');
    }
    else {
      return false
    }
  }
  
  function trackForm(event) {
  	var emailForm = event.target['email'];
    var email = emailForm ? window.form_extract_value(emailForm).toLocaleLowerCase() : '';
    if (email) {  
      console.log('Any email form submit');
      console.log(email);
      dataLayer.push({'event':'email_entered', 'userEmail': email});
      if (window.Raven) {
        Raven.setUserContext({
          email: email,
          id: window.md5(email)
        });
      }

      if (window.mixpanel) {
       var formLocation = window.location.href.split('/')[window.location.href.split('/').length-1];
       var props = {
          'itIp': window.ip,
          'action name': 'request '+ formLocation + ' link',
        };
        var userProps = {
          '$email': email,
        };
        if (isFirstTimeVizit()) {
            mixpanel.alias(window.md5(email));
            mixpanel.register({first_time: false});
            mixpanel.register({UserWasCreated: true});
          }
          else {
              mixpanel.identify(window.md5(email));
          }
          mixpanel.people.set(userProps);
          props = {'itIp': window.ip}
          mixpanel.track(
            'Form submit',
            Object.assign({}, props, userProps)
          );
      }
    }
  }
  
  
  
  forms = document.getElementsByTagName('form');
  for (i=0; i<forms.length; i++){
    forms[i].addEventListener('submit', trackForm);
  }

</script>

<script>
window.form_extract_value = function (form_field) {
  if (!form_field.length){
      return form_field.value
  }
  else {
      var elems = [];
	  form_field[0].form.email.forEach(function (elem){
      elems.push(elem.value);
    })
    result = elems.filter(function(elem){  
      return elem;
    })[0];
    return result;
  }
}
</script>

<script type="text/javascript">
  function getSearchParameters() {
    var prmstr = window.localStorage.getItem('adsUrlParams');
    return prmstr != null && prmstr != "" ? transformToAssocArray(prmstr) : {};
  }
  
  function tryToGetMixpanelProperty(name){
  	var property = undefined;
    if (window.mixpanel) {
      if (mixpanel.get_property){
      	property = mixpanel.get_property(name);
      }
      else {
      	setTimeout(getMixpanelProperty(name), 3000)
      }
    }
    return property
  }
  
  function getMixpanelProperty(name) {
    property = undefined;
  	if (mixpanel.get_property) {
    	property = mixpanel.get_property(name)
    }
    return property
  }	
  
  function transformToAssocArray(prmstr) {
      var params = {};
      var prmarr = prmstr.split("&");
      for ( var i = 0; i < prmarr.length; i++) {
          var tmparr = prmarr[i].split("=");
          params[tmparr[0]] = tmparr[1];
      }
      return params;
  }
  
  function setInitialReferrer() {
  	if (document.referrer.indexOf('https://improvado.io') == -1 && document.referrer != ''){
		localStorage.setItem('initial_referrer', document.referrer);
	}
  }
  
  function setForms() {
    var forms = document.getElementsByTagName('form');
    var utms = getSearchParameters();
    for (i = 0; i < forms.length; i++) {
      if (Object.keys(utms).length) {
	  //Set utms to form
	  delete utms.email
      for (var prop in utms) {
        inputElem = document.createElement("INPUT");
		inputElem.setAttribute("type", "hidden");
        inputElem.setAttribute("name", prop);
        inputElem.value = decodeURIComponent(utms[prop]);
        forms[i].appendChild(inputElem);
      }}
      else {
        inputElem = document.createElement("INPUT");
		inputElem.setAttribute("type", "hidden");
        inputElem.setAttribute("name", "utm_source");
        inputElem.value = 'Unknown';
        forms[i].appendChild(inputElem);
      }
      //Set other field to form
      var initial_refferer = localStorage.getItem('initial_referrer');
      initial_refferer = initial_refferer ? initial_refferer : '';
      var additionalInputs = {
        initial_referrer: initial_refferer,
        initial_referrer_domain: initial_refferer ? new URL(initial_refferer).hostname : '',
        referrer: document.referrer,
        referrer_domain: document.referrer ? new URL(document.referrer).hostname : '',
        initial_url: document.URL,
        last_url_part: new URL(document.URL).pathname.split('/').pop(),
        timezone_offset: new Date().getTimezoneOffset()/60*-1
      }
      for (var prop in additionalInputs) {
      	inputElem = document.createElement("INPUT");
		inputElem.setAttribute("type", "hidden");
        inputElem.setAttribute("name", prop);
        inputElem.value = additionalInputs[prop];
        forms[i].appendChild(inputElem);
      }
    }
  }
  setInitialReferrer();
  if (document.location.pathname !== '/dls/embed') {
    setForms();
  }
</script>
